Brilliant Energy. Dangerous Direction.

Van Gogh felt everything intensely. That's what made him a genius—2,100 paintings in a decade. But when stress overwhelmed him, that same intensity had nowhere to go. It turned inward. He cut off his ear. His energy spiraled into self-harm instead of art.

His problem wasn't big feelings. It was big feelings with no direction.

Here's why it happens: Stress shuts down the Default Mode Network—the part of your brain where creativity lives. When your nervous system is flooded, you can't think. You can't create. So that intense energy has nowhere to go but inward.

Your sensitivity is your superpower. Unmanaged, it becomes a trap.

3 Steps: Realize. Reset. Refocus.

Step 1 — REALIZE

Your Apple Watch detects stress before you do. When your nervous system activates (HRV drops), the app alerts you instantly. This is the moment. Not tomorrow. Now.

Step 2 — RESET

42 seconds of guided breathing (4-in, 6-out) with haptic cues on your wrist. Your nervous system returns. Your energy stabilizes.

Step 3 — REFOCUS

An exercise that brings you back to the present time and place. Look around. Notice what's real right now—your feet on the ground, your hands, the light, the air. You're anchored. Then capture that intensity—voice memo, text, whatever flows. The app learns when you're most creative and prompts you at the perfect moment.

Redirect Energy. Don't Suppress It.
